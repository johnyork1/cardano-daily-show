Bitcoin ETF Outflows Hit $818M in Single Day, Breaking Multi-Month Inflow Streak

BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust led the exodus with $318M in redemptions as institutional sentiment shifts to risk-off.
By Frank Chaparro · Feb 4, 2026 · 4 min read

Bitcoin spot ETFs experienced their largest single-day outflow since launch, with $818 million exiting the funds on January 29, breaking a multi-month inflow streak that had defined much of late 2025.

BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT), the largest Bitcoin ETF by assets, led the exodus with $318 million in redemptions, according to data from BitMEX Research. Fidelity's FBTC saw $215 million in outflows, while ARK 21Shares' ARKB recorded $142 million leaving the fund.

"This marks a clear shift from accumulation to risk-off behavior," said James Butterfill, head of research at CoinShares. "Institutional investors are treating Bitcoin like any other risk asset during periods of uncertainty."

The outflows pushed Bitcoin below the key $84,000 support level, triggering further selling from leveraged traders. Open interest in Bitcoin futures fell by $1.2 billion in the same 24-hour period.

Despite the recent weakness, total assets in U.S. Bitcoin ETFs remain substantial at approximately $58 billion, down from a peak of $78 billion in November 2025.
