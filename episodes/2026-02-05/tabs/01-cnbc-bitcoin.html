Bitcoin briefly breaks below $73,000 to lowest since November 2024 as heavy selling resumes

PUBLISHED TUE, FEB 3 2026 2:09 PM EST | UPDATED WED, FEB 4 2026 10:26 AM EST
Liz Napolitano
@LIZKNAPOLITANO

KEY POINTS

  • Bitcoin briefly dived below the $73,000 mark on Tuesday as investors have continued to rotate out of risk-on assets amid mounting geopolitical concerns.
  • The world's oldest cryptocurrency sank as low as $72,884.38, its lowest level since Nov. 6, 2024, when the token traded at $68,898.
  • Bitcoin has fallen 16% year to date as investors continue rotating out of risk assets amid geopolitical uncertainty.

Bitcoin tumbled below $73,000 on Tuesday, extending a brutal selloff that has wiped out much of the gains from last year's historic rally to all-time highs above $126,000.

The flagship cryptocurrency fell as much as 6.2% to $72,884, marking its lowest point since November 6, 2024 — the day before the U.S. presidential election that many in the crypto industry had bet would prove bullish for digital assets.

The selloff comes as risk appetite has evaporated across global markets, with investors fleeing to safe havens amid escalating geopolitical tensions and concerns about the trajectory of U.S. interest rates.

"What we're witnessing is a classic risk-off rotation," said Marcus Chen, head of digital assets at Fidelity Digital Assets. "Crypto is being treated like a high-beta tech stock — first to sell when investors get nervous."

Bitcoin is now down approximately 40% from its all-time high of $126,080 reached in October 2025. The total cryptocurrency market has shed more than $410 billion in value since the start of the year.
