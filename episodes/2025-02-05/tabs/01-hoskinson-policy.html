Charles Hoskinson slams Trump's crypto policy as 'extractive,' warns of industry fallout

Cardano founder says Trump's actions have politicized crypto and alienated half the country.

By Aoyon Ashraf, Jennifer Sanasie ¬∑ Jan 12, 2026

Charles Hoskinson, founder of Cardano and co-founder of Ethereum, has criticized the Trump administration's approach to cryptocurrency regulation, calling it "extractive" and warning it could cause long-term damage to the industry.

Key Point: Hoskinson argues that the administration's policies have turned cryptocurrency into a partisan issue, potentially alienating half of potential adopters.

Speaking at a recent industry conference, Hoskinson expressed concern that current regulatory approaches prioritize short-term political gains over sustainable industry growth.

"When you make crypto a partisan football, you've already lost half the country. That's not how you build a financial revolution."

The Cardano founder emphasized that blockchain technology should transcend political divisions, serving as infrastructure for all Americans regardless of their political affiliation.

Hoskinson called for a more balanced regulatory framework that protects consumers while allowing innovation to flourish, suggesting that the current approach may push development overseas.
