CME Group to offer cardano, chainlink, stellar futures as institutions seek regulated risk-management tools

The new futures contracts, available in micro and standard sizes, are set to launch on Feb. 9, pending regulatory approval.

By Omkar Godbole | Edited by Sam Reynolds

Updated Jan 16, 2026, 12:01 a.m. ¬∑ Published Jan 15, 2026, 11:47 p.m.

CME Group, the world's leading derivatives marketplace, announced plans to launch futures contracts for Cardano (ADA), Chainlink (LINK), and Stellar (XLM) as institutional demand for regulated cryptocurrency exposure continues to grow.

Key Highlight: The new contracts will be available in both micro and standard sizes, launching February 9, 2026, pending regulatory approval from the CFTC.

The move represents a significant expansion of CME's cryptocurrency derivatives offerings, which currently include Bitcoin and Ethereum futures. According to the exchange, the addition reflects growing institutional appetite for regulated exposure to a broader range of digital assets.

"As institutional interest in digital assets continues to evolve, we're committed to providing the risk-management tools our clients need," said Giovanni Vicioso, CME Group's Global Head of Cryptocurrency Products.

Cardano, in particular, has seen increased institutional interest following the launch of several spot ETF applications. The ADA futures contracts will allow institutions to hedge positions or gain exposure without holding the underlying asset directly.

The micro contracts will be sized at 1,000 ADA, 50 LINK, and 10,000 XLM respectively, while standard contracts will be 10x larger, catering to different trading strategies and risk appetites.
